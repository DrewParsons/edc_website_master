Join Us

It takes a city to build a home

We want our team to reflect the diverse set of residents EDC seeks to serve, which means we’re looking for movers and shakers from all across Evanston to join our project. Because we are collectively owned and give every member a voice at our table, we’re able to create a uniquely friendly, authentic community that values each of its members – whether you’re an employee, investor or advocate.

Why Join Our Community?

When you support our mission, you’re given the same voting power regardless of your investment: that means employees, people who’ve invested $1,000, and those who’ve invested $10,000 are equally involved in business decisions that impact them. Using this cooperative model, we promote collaboration across different stakeholders to ensure the entire community works towards long-standing issues of sensible housing, workforce development and environmental sustainability.

Sensible housing

Evanston residents are experiencing a growing mismatch between housing sizes and needs as homes are becoming bigger and more expensive. When aging couples want to downsize or young families hope to buy their first homes, they have few flexible options within the city. EDC homes fill this growing gap.

Workforce development

EDC recognizes two major concurrent issues impacting our community: First, there is a high unemployment rate among Evanston’s youth; and second, there is a shortage of trained construction workers throughout Illinois. By providing green construction job training to young Evanstonians, EDC offers paths to either well-paid, secure construction jobs or memberships to the cooperative.

Environmental sustainability

Last year, Evanston announced its plan to transition entirely to renewable energy by 2030 in its Climate Action and Resiliency Plan. Because the built environment is responsible for the greatest fraction of energy use, we need to build homes that minimize energy consumption. EDC homes will be high-quality and resilient, reducing costs and environmental footprints for residents.

Careers

EDC is always looking to provide professional opportunities for committed Evanstonians. Green building requires an enormous variety of skills, and we need the combined efforts of many talented residents to meet our goals. While the list of job descriptions below is by no means exhaustive, it speaks to the many ways you can get involved.

Builder

Builders oversee, coordinate and work on the construction of EDC coach houses and housing units. We also offer green construction training.

Architect

We are looking to create a team of contract architects to work with our homeowners in customizing beautiful, green, sensible homes.

Roofer

Roofers cover EDC homes with sturdy materials and work with electricians to integrate solar panels when necessary

Plumber

Plumbers assist in mapping the layouts for pipes, drainage systems and other materials, and then installs them in the home.

Electrician

Electricians at EDC design and install lighting and electrical systems and work with roofers to ensure solar panels are installed properly.

Attorney

EDC often needs assistance creating and reading contracts, and lobbying legislators for changes to building codes.

Accountant

Accountants consider tax implications for both our cooperative and our homeowners, researching and analyzing financial data at EDC.

Financial advisor

We are always looking for input on ways our investment mechanisms and business can grow and expand.

How Can You Help?

As we get closer to breaking ground on our first pilot houses, we need lots of help – with funding the project, with identifying potential employees, and with business processes. Reach out to us to learn how you can use your expertise to become a member of EDC, or benefit from construction job training opportunities as a young Evanstonian, with paths to either well-paid, secure construction jobs or memberships to the cooperative.
